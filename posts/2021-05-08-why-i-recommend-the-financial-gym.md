So one of my friends recently mentioned feeling overwhelmed by her finances. After hearing her out, I told her that I am a member of [The Financial Gym](https://financialgym.com/), and recommended that she reach out to them. 

For those not in the know: The Financial Gym provides holistic financial coaching. For a flat monthly fee, I gain access to a professional coach who will help me define my financial goals, and create a plan that translates my dreams for the future into my actions for today. 

My friend was surprised that I make use of such a service. The reason she even told me about any of her troubles is that she knows I'm really into personal finance.

I should mention at this point that I have read dozens of personal finance books and listened to hundreds of hours of podcasts on the topic. I meticulously track a variety of metrics around my net worth, investment performance, and am constantly looking for ways to achieve my financial goals more effectively. I've even considered starting my own personal finance blog or podcast. Why, then, do I need a financial coach? Surely I don't need help?

Perhaps that's true. I know what steps I'm supposed to take to make sure I'm reasonably well prepared for retirement, and I can follow through on them. I'd *probably* be okay without financial coaching. 

I think about the Financial Gym the same way I think about Muhammad Ali's boxing coach. Ali knew what he was doing - he didn't technically *need* a boxing coach. But he had one anyway. In order to achieve his highest potential, he worked with a coach to keep himself focused on his end goals, to provide an outside perspective, to challenge his limiting beliefs, and to provide motivation when times get hard.

My relationship with money is...complicated. Having grown up poor, my natural inclination is to spend my money, and spend it quickly - I feel the need to spend on fun things before I *have* to spend on making ends meet. Even though I know what principles need to be followed in order to achieve financial success, I'm like a doctor who is trying to quit smoking. I still get cravings, and every once in a while I lose the battle with them. 

In those moments, I rely on my coach to keep me from getting discouraged. I rely on my coach to help me adjust my plans when unexpected expenses arise (a broken phone, a trip to the emergency room, etc). I rely on my coach to help me through the times when I'm feeling stressed about anything with my financial situation. Finally, I rely on my coach to help me figure out how to handle drastic changes to my financial situation - how to buy a house, how to merge finances with my partner, or how to handle a global pandemic.

Having that support structure in place has been invaluable, and has helped me avoid lifestyle creep as I have made more money over time. I have been able to hit all of my savings goals, and I haven't experienced the fear of "will this transaction go through?" in a really long time. While I could have accomplished this without the Financial Gym, it would have taken me longer, and I'd have had a lot more stumbles along the way. 

It's a huge comfort knowing that I've got someone in my corner cheering me on, who just wants to see me have the life I want. I'm not saying that you need them in your life, but I can say that your life will be markedly better if you bring them into your life and let them help you.
