I came across a thread recently on...Twitter? I think it was? Anyway, the people in the thread were comparing millenials' experience with home buying, paying for college, and a variety of other major life experiences to that of the Baby Boomers. 

In one camp, there were people saying, "well, there's these charts that show that Boomers had it worse, or about the same!" and in the other camp there were people saying "no, no, no, the Boomers had it way easier!" 

It was very dramatic, and people were being very passionate for their side of the argument. But all I could think was, "who gives a fuck?"

Like, okay, maybe the Boomers had it easier. Maybe they didn't. But isn't that just a distraction? Like, yeah, as a Millenial it feels kind of shitty when folks older than you don't take the time to understand your situation before telling you how to live your life. And it would be lovely if they understood that times have changed, and how. And the same goes for the other way - it would be great if we could understand where the Boomers were coming from so we could more fully understand why they're giving the advice they are. 

But at the end of the day, who cares who had it worse? It doesn't matter. It's just a distraction: something to argue about so that you can say "oh no my life is so much harder than theirs was, so I can't do what they did." 

FUCK.

THAT.

NOISE.

Maybe my life is harder, maybe I've got to work twice as hard to acheive what they did. Is it fair? No. But life isn't fair. I've had opportunities that others didn't, and others have opportunities that I didn't. So what? I can complain about it, or I can just accept where I'm at, figure out where I want to go, and then ask, "how do I get there?"

That's not to say that we can or should ignore the social context in which we exist. Just to say that complaining and making excuses about who did or didn't have it better doesn't change anything. If you want results, you have to ignore that stuff, and make a plan.

You can have excuses, or you can have results. You can't have both.
