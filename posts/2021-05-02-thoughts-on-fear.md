Good golly I'm nervous. I'm a pretty introverted guy by nature. Putting things out into the world for other people to see is scary, and I don't generally do it. 

When I was a kid, I was on a baseball team one year (the only year I played). I was okay at the game, but not fantastic. My team was also pretty hamstrung because one kid broke his nose by getting hit with a baseball, and three parents pulled their kids out, leaving us to play the season with only 8 kids. 

But that's beside the point. When I was at bat in practice, I had no trouble hitting the ball. I'd swing all day long, and hit and get on base more often than I struck out. 

But as soon as we were in a game, and all the parents and the other team were watching me, and it _counted_, I froze up. I'd watch the ball fly right by me and not swing. I didn't want to embarrass myself by swinging and missing. I was afraid of being seen as a failure. But what my younger self didn't realize is that by neglecting to even try, I _was_ failing: my goal was to get on base and play the game, and I was not doing that. 

That pattern is one that I've continued to follow through my life. In many aspects of my life, I've stunted my own growth by not even taking the swing. When there was a girl I liked, I made excuses for not asking her out. When applying to colleges, I didn't want to face the possibility that I might not be as smart as I thought I was, so I didn't apply to the Ivy League schools. I didn't interact with professors because I was worried they would think I wasn't good enough. I was so worried about being branded a failure that I didn't even try. 

So I'm attempting to change that. I'm going to write things, and publish them online. And maybe all of them will fail. Maybe they won't. But I'm done with not trying. I'm done with trying to hide the times that I fail. It prevents me from learning, and it prevents me from _actually_ getting the things I want. 

So expect to see writing about all sorts of stuff here. Some of it will be software engineering, while some of it will be personal finance, and yet more of it will be just me talking about stuff like this post was. No matter what, I'm here for the long haul.
